### ðŸ”Ž 5-4-3 Rule (Ethernet)
The **5-4-3 rule** is an old Ethernet design rule for repeater-based networks (like early 10Base-5 / 10Base-2).  
It states that between any two end stations there can be **at most 5 segments** connected by **4 repeaters**, and **only 3 of those segments may be populated (have hosts attached)**.  
This rule helps limit collision domains and ensure signal timing stays within specifications for early Ethernet topologies.
